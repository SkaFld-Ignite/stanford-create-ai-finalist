# **What Is the Skafld Ideation Methodology?**

The Skafld Ideation Methodology is a systematic way to solve problems by studying how *other* fields solved similar problems—before asking anyone what they think.

That's it. That's the core insight.

# **Why Does This Matter?**

Most projects fail because they start in one of two traps:

| The "Expert" Trap | The "Customer" Trap |
| :---- | :---- |
| "We know this industry, so we'll do what's always been done" | "We asked customers what they want, so we'll build exactly that" |
| Result: Incremental improvements at best | Result: Features nobody actually uses |

**The Skafld Ideation Methodology avoids both traps** by forcing you to look outside your industry first, then quantify everything before asking for opinions.

# **The Core Philosophy: "Research Wide, Validate Narrow"**

Traditional approach: IDEA → ASK PEOPLE → BUILD → HOPE IT WORKS

Skafld Ideation Methodology approach: PROBLEM → STUDY OTHER FIELDS → COMBINE PATTERNS → QUANTIFY VALUE → THEN ASK PEOPLE → BUILD WITH CONFIDENCE

The magic happens in the middle steps that most people skip.

# **Why We Teach the Craft, Not the Shortcut**

*"Give someone a fish and they eat for a day. Teach them to fish and they eat for a lifetime."*

This ancient wisdom has never been more relevant than in the age of AI.

## **The Prompt Fallacy**

There's a tempting shortcut: "Just give me the AI prompt that does this." It seems efficient. Copy a prompt, paste it, get results. But this approach fails for the same reason that copying someone else's business plan fails—you don't understand why it works, so you can't adapt when it doesn't.

| The Shortcut (Giving Fish) | The Craft (Teaching to Fish) |
| :---- | :---- |
| "Here's a prompt that works" | "Here's why this question reveals patterns" |
| Works once, maybe | Works forever, adapts to anything |
| Breaks when context changes | Improves as context changes |
| Creates dependence | Creates capability |
| You use the tool | You understand the tool |

## **AI Is Marble, Not Magic**

Think of AI as a quarry that produces unlimited marble. It can generate code, designs, analyses, and strategies in seconds. But **abundance without understanding produces noise, not breakthroughs.**

The sculptor's value isn't in having marble—everyone has access to the same quarry. The value is in knowing which strikes of the chisel reveal the form hidden inside. That requires:

* **Curiosity:** The drive to ask "why does this work?" not just "does this work?"  
* **Understanding:** Knowing the principles behind the patterns, not just the patterns themselves  
* **Judgment:** The ability to recognize when AI output is breakthrough vs. plausible nonsense  
* **Adaptation:** Skill in adjusting your approach when the first attempt fails

## **The Same Principle, Applied to AI**

The Skafld Ideation Methodology's core insight—research deeply before acting—applies equally to how you use AI:

| Naive AI Use | Skilled AI Use |
| :---- | :---- |
| "Write me a business plan" | "What patterns exist in how successful X companies solved Y problem?" |
| Accepts first output | Interrogates, challenges, iterates |
| Asks for answers | Asks for reasoning |
| Uses AI to replace thinking | Uses AI to amplify thinking |
| Gets generic results | Gets breakthrough insights |

**The goal isn't to memorize prompts. The goal is to develop the curiosity and understanding that makes every prompt you write more powerful than the last.**

## **What We're Really Teaching**

When you learn the Skafld Ideation Methodology, you're not learning a checklist. You're developing:

* **A way of seeing:** The habit of looking beyond your industry for solutions  
* **A way of questioning:** The instinct to ask "who else has solved this?" before "what do we think?"  
* **A way of validating:** The discipline to quantify before you commit  
* **A way of learning:** The humility to let evidence change your mind

These capabilities compound. Every project makes you better at the next one. Every AI interaction teaches you something about how to have a better one. That's the difference between using a methodology and understanding it.

# **The 11 Phases (In Plain English)**

## **Phase 0: META-FRAMEWORK**

*"What game are we playing?"*

Before diving in, establish the rules:

* What does success look like?  
* What are our actual constraints?  
* Who will make the final decision?  
* How will we know if this works?

**Time:** 30-60 minutes

## **Phase 1: DECOMPOSE**

*"Break the problem into pieces you can actually solve"*

Use the 6-Box Canvas:

| WHAT | WHY | HOW |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| What's the actual problem? | Why does it exist? | How does it happen? |

| WHEN | WHERE | WHO |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| When does it occur? | Where is it happening? | Who's affected? |

**The Critical Move:** Separate real constraints from assumed constraints.

**Time:** 1-2 hours

## **Phase 2: RESEARCH**

*"How do OTHER fields solve this?"*

This is where the Skafld Ideation Methodology gets its power. You research 5 different types of domains:

| Domain Type | What It Means | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Adjacent | Same problem, different industry | How do gyms keep members? |
| Analogous | Different problem, similar structure | How does the military maintain unit cohesion? |
| Inverse | The opposite problem | How do dating apps help people leave successfully? |
| Extreme | Same problem, extreme conditions | How do emergency rooms prioritize patients? |
| Historical | How was this solved before? | How did medieval guilds retain apprentices? |

**Time:** 3-5 hours (highest investment phase)

## **Phase 2.5: SYNTHESIZE**

*"Combine patterns into something new"*

Now you have 15-25 patterns from different fields. Ask which patterns complement each other, what happens if you combine them, and what would be "impossible" but worth exploring.

**Time:** 2-3 hours

## **Phase 3: ARCHITECT**

*"Design THREE different solutions"*

Always create three options:

| Architecture | Risk Level | Innovation | When to Use |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Conservative | Low | Incremental | Need quick wins, risk-averse stakeholders |
| Balanced | Medium | Moderate | Most situations |
| Breakthrough | High | Transformative | Have runway, seeking market leadership |

**Time:** 2-4 hours

## **Phase 3.5: PRE-MORTEM**

*"Imagine it's one year later and this failed catastrophically. Why?"*

For each architecture, identify technical failures, market rejection scenarios, resource exhaustion risks, and external disruptions. Then work backwards to find early warning signs and prevention strategies.

**Time:** 1-2 hours

## **Phase 4: MODEL**

*"Put numbers on everything"*

This is where the Synthetic Balance Sheet comes in—a 4-quadrant value model:

|  | VALUE | COST |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| IMMEDIATE (0-12 mo) | Revenue, Cost savings, Risk mitigation | Development, Implementation, Opportunity cost |
| FUTURE (12+ mo) | Market position, Platform value, Strategic options | Technical debt, Maintenance, Obsolescence |

**Time:** 2-3 hours

## **Phase 4.5: STRESS TEST**

*"What happens when things go wrong?"*

Test each architecture against: 50% budget cut, timeline cut in half, key team member leaves, competitor copies you, regulation changes. Ask if the architecture survives and what breaks first.

**Time:** 1-2 hours

## **Phase 5: VALIDATE**

*"Now—and only now—get feedback"*

Get input from three types of validators:

| Validator Type | Who They Are | What They Test |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Domain Expert | Knows the field deeply | Is this feasible? |
| Anti-Expert | Skeptic, devil's advocate | Where are the holes? |
| Naive Expert | Smart but unfamiliar | Is this understandable? |

**Critical Rule:** If validation reveals fundamental problems, go back to earlier phases. This is called recursion and it's a feature, not a bug.

**Time:** 1-2 weeks

## **Phase 6: EXECUTE**

*"Build with continuous learning"*

Start with a Minimum Viable Experiment (MVE). Define clear "kill criteria" (when to stop) and "scale triggers" (when to expand). Capture learnings constantly.

**Time:** Ongoing

## **Phase 7: EVOLVE**

*"Feed learnings back into the system"*

Document what worked vs. what didn't, which patterns were most effective, what assumptions were wrong, and how the methodology should improve.

**Time:** Ongoing

# **Cross-Domain Research: The Heart of the Methodology**

## **Why It Works**

When you only look at your own industry, you see "best practices" (which means everyone does it), competitors' solutions (which you can't differentiate from), and incremental improvements (which don't create breakthroughs).

When you look at other fields, you see novel mechanisms that nobody in your industry has tried, proven solutions that just need translation, and combination opportunities that create true innovation.

## **How to Do Cross-Domain Research**

1. **Identify your core problem mechanism.** Don't research "how to improve customer retention." Instead, research "how to maintain long-term commitment relationships."  
2. **Find 5 domains that deal with this mechanism.** Look for adjacent, analogous, inverse, extreme, and historical examples.  
3. **Extract transferable patterns.** For each domain: How do they solve it? Why does that work? What's the core principle? Could that principle apply to us?  
4. **Look for surprising combinations.** The best innovations often come from combining patterns that "shouldn't" work together.

# **Working With AI: The Same Principles Apply**

AI is the most powerful tool available for cross-domain research and pattern synthesis. But like any tool, its value depends entirely on how you use it.

## **The Curiosity Requirement**

Effective AI use requires the same mindset as the methodology itself:

* **Ask "why" before "what."** "Why do gaming companies achieve 90% daily engagement?" beats "How do I increase engagement?"  
* **Seek patterns, not answers.** "What patterns exist across industries that solved X?" beats "What's the solution to X?"  
* **Challenge outputs.** "What would make this wrong?" and "What am I missing?" are essential follow-ups.  
* **Iterate with purpose.** Each interaction should build on the last, going deeper rather than starting over.

## **Questions That Unlock AI's Potential**

| Instead of... | Try... |
| :---- | :---- |
| "Write a marketing plan" | "What unconventional channels did successful startups in \[adjacent field\] use to acquire their first 1000 customers?" |
| "How do I reduce churn?" | "How do subscription-based businesses in gaming, fitness, and media solve the month-3 dropout problem?" |
| "Give me ideas" | "What would a solution look like if we combined \[Pattern A\] from military logistics with \[Pattern B\] from behavioral psychology?" |
| "Is this a good idea?" | "What are the three most likely ways this fails, and who has solved each failure mode?" |

The difference isn't in the AI—it's in you. Curiosity and understanding turn AI from an answer machine into a thinking partner.

# **Common Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)**

| Mistake | Why It Happens | The Fix |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Skipping the Research Phase | "We already know our industry" | You know your industry—that's the problem. Study 3+ unrelated fields. |
| Getting Stakeholder Input Too Early | "We want to be customer-centric" | Research first, validate later. Early opinions anchor your thinking. |
| Only Creating One Architecture | "We know which approach is best" | Always create three. The "backup" options often become winners. |
| Weak Value Quantification | "The benefits are hard to measure" | If you can't quantify it, you can't decide. Put a number on everything. |
| Skipping Pre-Mortem | "We don't want to be negative" | Imagining failure before you build is the cheapest risk mitigation. |
| Treating Recursion as Failure | "We should have gotten it right" | Going back with new information is how breakthroughs happen. |
| Using AI for Answers, Not Understanding | "Just give me the prompt" | Develop curiosity first. Prompts without understanding produce noise. |

# **The Skafld Ideation Methodology Mindset**

**Traditional Mindset:** "I have a great idea. Let me convince people it will work."

**Skafld Ideation Methodology Mindset:** "I have a problem worth solving. Let me discover what actually works by studying every field I can find, then prove it with numbers before asking anyone's opinion."

The difference isn't just process—it's humility. The Skafld Ideation Methodology assumes you don't already know the best answer, and that the best answer probably exists in a field you haven't explored yet.
