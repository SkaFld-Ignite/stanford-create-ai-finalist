# SkaFld Trailhead — ESUSD Partnership Implementation Plan

## Overview

SkaFld Trailhead is a portfolio-based career pathway program where high school students build employer-validated portfolios by solving real problems for local companies using AI. Students develop durable metacognitive skills — decomposition thinking, cross-domain pattern recognition, and responsible AI practices — that transfer across any tool or domain.

This plan outlines the implementation partnership between **SkaFld Studio LLC** and **El Segundo Unified School District** to pilot SkaFld Trailhead with 12 students over 12 months.

---

## Program at a Glance

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Students | 12 in 1 cross-grade team (9th-12th) |
| Teacher Mentor | 1 dedicated teacher mentor |
| Session Format | Monthly 90-minute after-school sessions, teacher-led |
| Employer Partners | 2-3 local companies (aerospace, tech) |
| Curriculum | SkaFld Ideation Methodology |
| Platform | SkaFld Trailhead (AI-powered, COPPA/FERPA compliant) |
| Duration | 12 months |

---

## Session Delivery Model

SkaFld prepares the teacher mentor to lead all monthly after-school sessions independently. SkaFld's role is to **train, equip, and support** — not to attend every session.

- **Teacher-Led**: The trained teacher mentor facilitates monthly after-school sessions using SkaFld's curriculum guides and the Trailhead platform. The teacher is the consistent presence for students.
- **SkaFld Support**: SkaFld provides ongoing coaching, curriculum materials, and platform support. SkaFld joins select sessions for key moments (project kickoffs, employer reviews, mid-year check-ins).
- **Scheduling**: Sessions are held monthly after school. Specific day-of-week and start time will be determined collaboratively with the school to fit existing schedules and student needs.

## Responsibility Matrix

### What SkaFld Provides

- **SkaFld Trailhead Platform** — AI Coaching Engine, Portfolio Review System, Student Progression Tracking, Teacher Dashboard. Fully COPPA/FERPA compliant with enterprise SSO (Microsoft/Google).
- **Curriculum & Training** — Complete SkaFld Ideation Methodology curriculum designed for teacher-led monthly after-school sessions. SkaFld trains the teacher mentor to deliver the program independently, with session guides, facilitation materials, and ongoing coaching support. SkaFld does not attend every session — the teacher leads.
- **Periodic Direct Support** — SkaFld joins select sessions for key milestones (e.g., project kickoffs, employer portfolio reviews, mid-year check-ins). The specific cadence of SkaFld-attended sessions will be determined collaboratively with the school.
- **Employer Partnerships** — Recruitment and onboarding of 2-3 employer partners in El Segundo's aerospace/tech corridor for project briefs and portfolio review.
- **Program Management** — Coordination, progress tracking, employer communications, quarterly portfolio review facilitation. Day-to-day session delivery is led by the trained teacher mentor.
- **Assessment & Reporting** — Learning outcome tracking with disaggregated equity monitoring. Progress reports and year-end assessment.

### What ESUSD Provides

- **District Point of Contact** — A designated coordinator (CTE coordinator, assistant principal, or equivalent) to serve as the primary liaison between SkaFld and the district.
- **Facility Access** — A classroom or meeting space for monthly after-school sessions. Specific day and start time will be determined collaboratively between SkaFld and the school.
- **Teacher Mentor** — 1 teacher mentor who serves as the primary session facilitator. SkaFld provides comprehensive training, session-by-session curriculum guides, and ongoing coaching so the teacher can lead monthly sessions independently. The teacher brings their knowledge of students and school culture; SkaFld equips them with the methodology and materials.
- **Student Recruitment Support** — Help promoting the program to students and families and processing parental consent forms.
- **Administrative Support** — Standard district approvals, data sharing agreements, and coordination with existing CTE or after-school programming.
