# Budget Justification
## AI Studio Teams — Subrecipient: SkaFld Studio LLC

**Period of Performance:** March 1, 2026 – February 28, 2027
**Total Subaward Amount:** $20,000

---

## Project Funding Context

The $20,000 subaward from Stanford Accelerator for Learning represents foundational funding for the AI Studio Teams project. This award will be combined with additional funds raised from complementary sources — including district CTE budget allocations, employer partnership contributions, workforce development grants, and in-kind employer support — to fully capitalize the project. The Stanford CREATE+AI award serves as the catalytic investment that validates the model and unlocks these additional funding commitments.

---

## A. Personnel (Contract Engineering) — $6,000

**Contract Software Engineer(s), ~120 hours @ $50/hr: $6,000**

Contract engineering resources are required to complete the SkaFld Trailhead platform features necessary for the El Segundo pilot launch. Specific deliverables include:

- **AI Coaching Engine:** Completing the Socratic questioning module that guides students through problem decomposition — the core pedagogical mechanism of the program.
- **Portfolio Review System:** Building the employer-facing portal where industry partners securely view, rate, and provide feedback on student work samples.
- **Student Progression Tracking:** Finalizing the gamified learning journey system that tracks student progress through the 12-week curriculum phases (Foundation & Fluency, Increasing Complexity, Real-World Mastery).

These are specialized development tasks that require dedicated engineering time beyond what the volunteer core team can absorb while managing program design, partnerships, and operations.

**Core team personnel — Charles Sims (Project Director, 50% effort), Keith Coleman (Strategy & Partnerships, 40% effort), and Mike Belloli (Technology & Operations, 30% effort) — contribute their professional time on a volunteer/in-kind basis**, representing significant leveraged value for the Stanford investment. This structure ensures that the vast majority of the subaward funds directly into technology infrastructure and pilot execution rather than administrative overhead.

## B. Technology & Platform Development — $10,000

This is the highest-leverage allocation in the budget. By investing in platform infrastructure, the project moves from a service-heavy consulting model to a scalable technology product that can serve thousands of districts.

### Cloud Infrastructure & Hosting — $3,000

Application hosting, database services, CDN, and compute resources to support the 12-month pilot serving 96 students, 8 teacher mentors, and 12 employer partners. Includes production environment, staging/testing environments, and automated backup and recovery systems.

### AI/LLM API Costs — $3,000

API usage fees for the large language model powering the Trailhead AI Coach — the Socratic questioning engine that guides students through problem decomposition and cross-domain research. The AI Coach is the program's core differentiator: it teaches students *how to think* about problems rather than providing answers, building durable metacognitive skills that transfer across any AI tool. Estimated usage is based on 96 students generating approximately 4 coaching sessions per week over 40 program weeks.

### Software Licenses & Development Tools — $2,000

Development tooling including CI/CD pipeline services, automated testing infrastructure, application monitoring, and error tracking. These tools ensure platform reliability and rapid iteration during the pilot — critical when 96 students and 12 employers depend on the system for weekly sessions.

### Security & Compliance Tooling — $2,000

COPPA and FERPA compliance is non-negotiable for a K-12 platform handling student data. This allocation covers:
- Consent management and parental authorization systems
- Data encryption at rest and in transit
- AI-powered content moderation with human review escalation
- Enterprise SSO integration (Microsoft and Google) for district authentication
- Security auditing and vulnerability scanning

## C. Travel — $2,000

### Stanford SAL Convenings and Meetings — $1,500 (2 trips)

Team members will travel to Stanford University for Accelerator for Learning convenings and PI coordination meetings. These in-person interactions are essential for maintaining alignment with Stanford's research framework and reporting requirements. Each trip includes:
- Airfare or mileage (estimated $400/trip)
- Lodging, 1 night (estimated $200/trip)
- Per diem and ground transportation (estimated $150/trip)

### El Segundo Pilot Site Coordination — $500 (2 trips)

Travel for employer onboarding and school district coordination meetings at the El Segundo pilot site. Includes mileage, parking, and meeting expenses.

## D. Other Direct Costs — $2,000

### Curriculum & Assessment Materials — $1,000

Adapting the SkaFld Ideation Methodology ("Research Wide, Validate Narrow") into classroom-ready guides for teacher mentors. Includes development of the Employer Portfolio Review Rubric (covering Work Quality, AI Tool Utilization, Responsible AI Practices, Problem-Solving, Communication, and Collaboration dimensions) and assessment instruments for tracking decomposition thinking and AI literacy gains.

### Employer Onboarding & Communications — $500

Professional materials for the 12 employer partner organizations, including onboarding packets, partnership agreements, Employer Council governance documentation, and program collateral. Credible materials are necessary when engaging aerospace and defense employers (Boeing, Northrop Grumman) who expect formal partnership documentation.

### Supplies & Operational Expenses — $500

Administrative costs including document management, secure communication tools for coordinating across the project team, school district, and employer partners.

## E. Indirect Costs (F&A) — $0

Indirect costs will not be charged on this project. All costs represent direct expenditures in support of the project activities described in the Statement of Work.

---

## Cost Efficiency and Leverage

The $20,000 Stanford subaward is designed to be catalytic. With the core leadership team contributing their professional effort on a volunteer basis, over 80% of the award flows directly into technology infrastructure, platform development, and pilot execution — not overhead.

Combined with additional funds being raised, the full AI Studio Teams project delivers a working pilot at approximately $521 per student (96 students), a price point deliberately designed to be sustainable through district CTE budgets and employer co-investment in Year 2 and beyond. The Stanford CREATE+AI award is the first dollar — and the hardest — that makes the rest possible.
