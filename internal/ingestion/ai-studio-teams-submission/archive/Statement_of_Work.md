## 1. Background and Purpose

AI Studio Teams is a portfolio-based career pathway program that addresses the "Experience Paradox" — the disappearance of entry-level positions that traditionally provided young people their first professional experience. The program creates structured pathways where high school students build employer-validated portfolios by solving real problems for local companies using AI, developing durable metacognitive skills that transfer across any AI tool or domain.

The program will pilot in El Segundo, California, serving 96 students across 8 teams of 12, partnering with 12 local employer organizations (including aerospace and technology firms), and deploying the SkaFld Trailhead AI-powered learning platform.

This $20,000 subaward represents foundational funding that will be combined with additional funds raised from district CTE allocations, employer partnerships, workforce development grants, and in-kind contributions to fully execute the Year 1 pilot.

## 2. Scope of Work

SkaFld Studio LLC will execute the following activities under the direction of Subrecipient PI Keith Coleman, with support from the core team (Charles Sims, Project Director; Mike Belloli, Technology & Operations).

### 2.1 Platform Development and Technology Infrastructure (Months 1–6)

- Complete development of the SkaFld Trailhead platform for pilot deployment, including:
  - **AI Coaching Engine:** Socratic questioning module guiding students through problem decomposition and cross-domain research using the SkaFld Ideation Methodology ("Research Wide, Validate Narrow").
  - **Portfolio System:** Employer-facing review portal enabling industry partners to view, rate, and validate student work samples against defined rubric criteria.
  - **Student Journey:** Gamified progression paths tracking student advancement through three curriculum phases: Foundation & Fluency (Weeks 1–4), Increasing Complexity (Weeks 5–8), and Real-World Mastery (Weeks 9–12).
  - **Teacher Dashboard:** Real-time visibility into student progress for teacher mentors.
- Implement COPPA/FERPA compliance infrastructure including consent management, data encryption, content moderation, and enterprise SSO integration.
- Deploy and maintain cloud infrastructure for 12-month pilot operations.

### 2.2 Employer Partnership Development (Months 1–6)

- Recruit and onboard 12 employer partners in El Segundo's aerospace and technology corridor to provide authentic project briefs for student teams.
- Develop structured employer engagement frameworks including quarterly review panels and micro-internship design (10–20 hour paid experiences).
- Establish the Employer Council governance structure to provide ongoing guidance on portfolio standards and workforce readiness criteria.

### 2.3 Pilot Implementation and Program Delivery (Months 4–12)

- Coordinate the El Segundo pilot launch: student recruitment, team formation, and teacher mentor preparation.
- Adapt the SkaFld Ideation Methodology into classroom-ready curriculum guides for 8 teacher mentors leading weekly 90-minute sessions.
- Manage program logistics including session scheduling, employer project coordination, and portfolio review cycles.
- Facilitate quarterly employer review panels where industry professionals evaluate student portfolios.

### 2.4 Stanford Collaboration and Research Alignment (Ongoing, Months 1–12)

- Serve as liaison between AI Studio Teams and Stanford Accelerator for Learning (SAL).
- Coordinate research alignment with Stanford's evaluation criteria: Innovation & Creativity, Learning Impact, Fairness & Inclusion, Use of Learning Sciences & Design, Measurement Plan, and Feasibility & Sustainability.
- Participate in SAL convenings, reporting, and knowledge-sharing activities.

### 2.5 Assessment, Measurement, and Equity Monitoring (Months 3–12)

- Implement learning outcome tracking for decomposition thinking and AI literacy gains.
- Manage the employer portfolio review process using defined rubric (Work Quality, AI Tool Utilization, Responsible AI Practices, Problem-Solving, Communication, Collaboration).
- Monitor equity targets with disaggregated tracking: 50% female/male, 40% free/reduced lunch, 30% first-generation college.
- Document research findings for future grant applications and policy contributions.

### 2.6 Sustainability and Expansion Planning (Months 7–12)

- Develop the Year 2+ sustainability model including district CTE budget integration and employer co-investment structures.
- Identify and pursue additional funding sources (workforce development grants, DOL, state workforce boards).
- Create strategic plan for Year 2 California expansion (5–10 districts) and Year 3 national scaling.
